The Story

From a lab in Nairobi to leading learner success across a continent

Nobody handed me this path. I kept showing up, kept volunteering, kept speaking, and the rooms kept getting bigger. Here is how it happened.

Chapter One · 2018 to 2020

The student who kept going back

Computer Science at the University of Nairobi. I walked into my first tech meetups understanding almost nothing. I kept going back anyway. Through the KamiLimu mentorship programme I found the pillar that would define my career: community engagement.

I became Developer Student Clubs Lead at UoN, an events organizer with GDG Nairobi, and a Moringa School Campus Ambassador. I learned that leadership is not about knowing everything. It is about a willingness to lead people in the direction you want to go, and learning right alongside them.

DSC Lead, University of Nairobi GDG Nairobi organizer KamiLimu Cohort 4.0 mentee

Chapter Two · 2020 to 2021

Google noticed

I discovered Crowdsource by Google at a Developer Student Clubs summit in Ghana and turned it into a mission: put Kenya, and Africa, on the map. I suggested the #30daysofCrowdsource challenge. Google rolled it out across Africa, Latin America, and Asia.

That energy became a rare contract role as Crowdsource Community Coordinator: the sole Kenya representative working with a team based in India, with access to the Google Nairobi office. I coordinated communities across 30+ universities in six East African countries, driving over 2 million contributions that improved products like Google Translate and Google Lens for African users. Google featured my story on the official Crowdsource blog.

2M+ contributions driven 30+ universities, 6 countries Featured on Google's Crowdsource blog

Chapter Three · 2021 to 2024

Building AltSchool's community from cohort one

After a recruiting internship at TalentQL, where I supported 50+ placements with over 90% data accuracy, I joined AltSchool Africa and built its learner community from the very first cohort. SOE v1 alone was around 4,000 learners. Tinyuka 2023 brought 4,645 across three schools. Tinyuka 2024 added 5,374 more. I designed the onboarding, engagement, student leadership, and feedback systems that carried all of them.

The work was rarely glamorous. Following up with 1,500+ unpaid learners over Slack and email. Escalating 420+ payment issues in a single cohort. Hosting 31 events in one year, with a LinkedIn Optimization session drawing 694 attendees and a Financial Literacy session drawing 400. Onboarding 55 campus ambassadors whose referrals fed 301 community-led acquisitions through the Earn Program. It added up: a 59% payment conversion on follow-ups, ₦10 million recovered in ISA payments through one reminder campaign, and a community learners genuinely loved.

20,000+ learners, 11 cohorts 59% payment conversion Employee of the Year & Brand Ambassador, 2023

Chapter Four · 2024 to now

Head of Learner Success

In October 2024 I stepped up to lead both the Community and Customer Support teams. I cut average human response time from 45 minutes 39 seconds to 36 minutes 03 seconds, a 21% improvement. I built the systems that work even when I am not in the room: an 11-tab Community Content Playbook with a 30-week calendar and a library of 38 reusable motivation messages, the AltSchool Community HQ indexing all 46 team documents, and a Cohort Command Centre tracking the full lifecycle of 7 cohorts from admissions to graduation. Along the way I completed McKinsey Forward in 2025 and joined the Aspire Leaders Program in 2026.

Then came sTaRt, AltSchool's first flagship conference in Lagos. I owned speaker communications, agendas, and live coordination for an event that drew over 1,000 people in person, 6,700 on the livestream, and 1,000+ more on YouTube. Mid-event, our CEO stopped the programme, took the mic, and asked the room to clap for the person who made it happen.

Short bio · for event programmes

Tabitha Kavyu is a community and learner success leader from Nairobi, Kenya, with over five years of experience building tech communities across Africa. As Head of Learner Success at AltSchool Africa, she leads the Community and Customer Support teams and has supported more than 20,000 learners across 11 cohorts, having built the community from its very first cohort. Before AltSchool, she served as Community Coordinator for Crowdsource by Google, the sole Kenya representative coordinating communities across 30+ universities in six East African countries and driving over 2 million contributions that improved Google products for African users. Her community leadership spans GDG Nairobi, Open Source Community Africa Nairobi, and the Google Developer Student Clubs. She holds a BSc in Computer Science from the University of Nairobi, a CMX Community Management MBA, and is an alumna of McKinsey Forward and the Aspire Leaders Program. She exists to use her voice, her story, and her presence to encourage people just starting out, so they believe in themselves, take their next step, and know that someone like them already did it.
